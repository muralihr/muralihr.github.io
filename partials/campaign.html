Clean Air Campaigns

Motor vehicles are a major source of pollution, which imposes significant human and financial costs on society. Cycling should therefore be encouraged as a way of reducing pollution. This would help the cities in India comply with its legal limits on air quality and improve public health.

Bicycle for Integration

The “Karnataka Darshana” was a bicycle tour that started on July 12 from Bidar and ended in Bengaluru on July 25, during which 15 cities, 25 districts, and important cultural heritage sites of Hampi, Moodbidri, Mysuru, Belur, and Vijayapura among others will be covered. As many as 55 members of the Karnataka State Reserve Police (KSRP), embarked on a 1,7650km ‘Karnataka Darshan’ cycling expedition across karnataka. NNCF was responsible for planning the cycling ride across the state and operations support was provided by NNCF. The group covered around 160 km a day and touched all major police centres in the state. Every district they visited, they interacted with the locals and school kids. The cyclists spoke about Swachh Bharat, Fitness, benefits of environment protection, safety of school children and road safety.
